About Me

Biography

Ever since I was a kid in Delaware, I knew I would have a career involving visual communications. In my early years, I would spend a lot of time drawing, mostly cartoon characters and portraits. In high school, I realized there wasn't much of a career path for an artist, so I pursued graphic design. From that point, I painstakingly polished my craft until I graduated with a degree in multimedia design. During that time, I began to look at the potential job market in Delaware and realized two things; I needed to know code and I needed to move. About a month after graduation, I landed a job with Collaboration Reverberation, as a Front End Dev and moved to sunny San Diego.

Accomplishments

D3

Working with code means there is always something new, always something to learn, always something around the corner. With this reality, myself and fellow developers need to stay sharp, often on their own time. In January of 2014, I was able to finalize a proposal that would allow our developers to do more learning on the job. We call it D3, every Friday after lunch, we are able to explore any development subject we like. This benefits the company ( happy developers, create reusable tools for the company ), as well as the developers ( professional development ).

@font-face

In November of 2013, after doing extensive research, I lead the way for CR's adoption of a font subscription. Things were getting pretty tough, with more and more custom fonts being used by our designers; that lead us to move away from Cufon. Now with our subscription, we have access to over 20,000 web fonts.

Device Lab
